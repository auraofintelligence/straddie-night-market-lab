# Partner Map

This is a starter map for conversations. Partner status begins at contact planning and moves forward through direct review.

## Venue Partners

- Point Lookout Bowls Club: existing market context and family/community market baseline.
- Little Ship Club: Dunwich waterfront, sunset and ferry-adjacent tests.
- The Straddie Hotel: late trade, live music, Sunday sessions and visitor concentration.
- Amity Point Community Club: Amity community music and family gathering potential.
- Point Lookout Community Hall: arts, projection and wet-weather style gatherings.
- Straddie Sharks / Ron Stark Oval: sports, family and community-event layer.

## Transport Partners

- SeaLink: vehicle ferry, passenger ferry, terminal notices, published timetables, service status, vessel allocation and known passenger/vehicle capacity.
- Gold Cats / Stradbroke Flyer: water taxi, One Mile Jetty, published timetable, mainland courtesy bus, walk-up ticket behaviour and vessel capacity to confirm.
- Stradbroke Island Buses: 880 and 881 route fit, published timetable, service updates, charter options and vehicle capacity.
- Stradbroke Cab Service: late-night taxi movement, accessible van option and 10-seat commuter van capacity.
- StradEasy: private booked hire, ferry-to-door transfer, up-to-11 passenger vehicles and group movement support.
- Venue courtesy buses and local transfer options: safe movement after dark and confirmed last-return advice.

## Data and Distribution Partners

- Straddie Noticeboard Network: public-safe notices, expiry dates, QR questions, venue-specific feeds.
- Straddie Media / news network: gig guide, weekend wrap, ferry-screen updates, local beat routing and post-event learning.
- Ready S.E.T. Co-op + Hyperlocal Media: training, help desk, local content rhythm, public proof and media skills pathway.
- Virtual Minjerribah / future simulation layer: visitor-flow, ferry choice, crowd comfort, weather and projection assumptions.
- Stradbroke Grants Lab: entity search, future evidence packs, milestones and acquittals.

## First Ask

Can we map existing schedules, approval paths, capacity limits and public FAQ needs before a tiny pilot is proposed?

## Better First Asks

- Ask which dates, times and locations are already approved.
- Ask which public details need verification before noticeboard use.
- Ask which aggregate data can be shared without personal details.
- Ask which transport FAQ answers reduce pressure on staff, visitors, ferry queues, buses and stallholders.
- Ask which disruption and backup details are needed before promotion: service alert links, last return, taxi/private transfer fallback, wet-weather cutoff and who updates changed details.
- Search the Grants Lab entities list before outreach.
- Route confirmed public stories through Ready S.E.T. Media when useful.
