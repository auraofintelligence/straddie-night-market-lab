# Research Notes

Last refreshed: 11 May 2026.

This is a quick scan for Straddie market onboarding. It supports applications, contact planning, host review and current schedule checks.

## Market Signals

- Point Lookout Bowls Club has public Sunday market material with local artisan, craft, food and community-stall language.
- North Stradbroke Island tourism material also points to Point Lookout Markets at the Bowls Club, historically tied to Sunday and holiday patterns.
- SeaLink's Point Lookout Markets listing describes the Bowls Club market as a tourist and local draw, with morning timing and stall contact details.

## Venue Signals

- The Straddie Hotel is the strongest late-trade candidate in the quick scan because its public page describes the public bar as open until late and mentions live music nights and Sunday sessions.
- Little Ship Club is more of a sunset or early-evening candidate. Public material shows live music events and waterfront value, but many published seasonal hours finish before full night-time.
- Amity Point Community Club has strong community fit and live-music/function signals, but transport after dark needs more care because it is further from the ferry gateway.
- Straddie Sharks / Ron Stark Oval has sports and community-event fit, especially around family or carnival patterns.

## Event and Culture Signals

- Redland City Council describes Quandamooka Festival as including art, music, dance, artisan markets, food stalls, storytelling, cultural heritage and caring for Country. This is a useful signal, but only cultural-authority-led activity should attach to that kind of event.
- Past Straddie Arts Trail coverage described a distributed arts pattern across Dunwich, Amity and Point Lookout, with many artists, groups and stops. That supports a trail or micro-market model across several host sites.
- Ubuntu Expo shows an arts, music and community gathering signal at Point Lookout Community Hall.

## Transport Notes

- Visit Straddie describes Stradbroke Flyer and SeaLink as different operators using different Dunwich arrival points.
- Stradbroke Flyer Gold Cat public material says it is a passenger ferry, roughly 25 minutes, with a free mainland bus service and no online bookings.
- SeaLink publishes vehicle and passenger ferry timetables, service status links and vessel-capacity details. Current fleet notes list Minjerribah at 52 cars / 400 passengers, Sea Breeze at 60 cars / 300 passengers, Quandamooka at 52 cars / 400 passengers and Yalingbila at 198 passengers.
- Stradbroke Island Buses publishes timetable links, 880 and 881 route information, and charter information with vehicles listed at 40 to 62 seats.
- Translink's 1 December 2025 bus update extended route 881 to Dunwich, added QUAMPI, added 880 services and introduced seasonal timetable capacity for school holidays, public holidays and weekends.
- Stradbroke Cab Service has a public listing for 2 x 10-seat commuter vans, including one wheelchair-accessible vehicle.
- StradEasy public material describes private booked hire, ferry-to-door transport from Dunwich and One Mile, and vehicles seating up to 11 passengers.
- Stradbroke Island Events notes ferries, buses, courtesy buses, taxis and transfer options. This supports a partner map around visitor flow and host comfort.
- Disruption and backup data needs to sit beside the timetable: SeaLink status, bus service updates, Flyer timetable changes, weather cutoff, last-return path, taxi/private transfer fallback and who updates changed public details.
