Public-safe workflow

A night market notice works best with a verification date.

This keeps the system useful during changing weather, ferry delays, venue changes or "we are still asking" phases.

---
title: Possible night market test
status: question gathering only
location: Little Ship Club
season_window: winter sunset
contributor_role: stallholder or projection contributor
public_boundary: host review required
expires: 2026-06-30
---

Ask now:
- Which twilight hours are realistic?
- Can visitors return safely?
- Is projection welcome or too much?
- Who verifies the final public notice?

Why markdown

Readable by people and agents.

A person can edit it. An agent team can validate it. A public screen can render it. If a fact changes, one simple file update can refresh the notice.

Future-forward layer

One small notice can become planning data.

QR answers, host updates, transport signals and stallholder questions can feed a larger loop: public notice, private planning note, simulation seed, media brief and later learning.

Planning seed

A venue idea becomes a small data packet: place, time, host, transport, projection, crowd comfort and open questions.

Simulation seed

The same packet can later support flow modelling, ferry timing comparisons, weather scenarios and event-capacity assumptions.

News seed

Confirmed public details can become a gig guide item, weekend wrap, ferry-screen update or post-event learning note.

Data collection

Useful questions with a light touch.

Visitors

  • Are you local, day-tripping or staying overnight?
  • Which boat or transport mode are you likely to use?
  • What time would you realistically leave?

Stallholders

  • What do you need: space, power, light, shade, table or online notice?
  • Are you food, craft, art, music, repair, tech help or community info?
  • Which seasons are worth your time?

Hosts

  • What is the safe capacity?
  • Which details need host approval before advertising?
  • Who can update a cancellation or change notice quickly?

Public notices are only as good as their verification.

The noticeboard bridge protects privacy, expiry dates and source checks.
