Public-safe workflow

A night market notice works best with a verification date.

This keeps the system useful during changing weather, ferry delays, venue changes or details still being confirmed.

---
title: Possible night market test
status: draft
next_step: host confirmation
location: Little Ship Club
season_window: winter sunset
contributor_role: stallholder or projection contributor
public_boundary: host review required
expires: 2026-06-30
---

Confirm before publishing:
- What date, time, setup and pack-down window is confirmed?
- Which transport and last-return details should visitors see?
- Which stallholder categories or needs are approved?
- Who updates cancellation or weather notices?

Why this draft exists

One notice, fewer mixed messages.

The draft keeps unconfirmed details out of public promotion. Once the host confirms time, transport and contact details, the same notice can be used on screens, phones and venue updates.

Future-forward layer

One small notice can become planning data.

QR answers, host updates, transport details and stallholder questions can feed a larger loop: public notice, private planning note, media brief and later learning.

Planning seed

A venue idea becomes a small data packet: place, time, host, transport, projection, crowd comfort and open questions.

Simulation seed

The same packet can later support flow modelling, ferry timing comparisons, weather scenarios and event-capacity assumptions.

News seed

Confirmed public details can become a gig guide item, weekend wrap, ferry-screen update or post-event learning note.

Data collection

Useful questions with a light touch.

Visitors

  • Are you local, day-tripping or staying overnight?
  • Which ferry, bus, car, walk or courtesy option will you use?
  • What arrival and departure window suits you?

Stallholders

  • What do you sell, share, demonstrate or provide?
  • What do you need: stall size, power, light, table, shelter, water or storage?
  • Which dates and times can you attend?

Hosts

  • Which spaces and times are approved for public notice?
  • What capacity, toilet, access and wet-weather limits apply?
  • Who updates cancellation, weather or transport changes?
