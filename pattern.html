The method

Onboard before promoting.

Every suggested location becomes a participation pathway: who hosts, who wants to contribute, which season fits, how people arrive and leave, whether light projection is welcome, and what the public noticeboard can safely share.

Use the mapper Open markdown questions

Working voice

Curious, useful, community-led.

The preferred sentence is: "What would participation look like here, and what do we need to know first?" That keeps the process flexible for Little Ship Club, Straddie Hotel, Amity Point Community Club, the Bowlsie, arts events, sports events and future suggestions.

Data loop

The useful output is better questions.

The loop is designed for the Straddie Noticeboard Network: gather a small signal, verify it, publish confirmed public information, then update or expire it cleanly.

1

Suggest

A venue, event or season is suggested by a local, a club, a stallholder, a visitor, a host or a partner.

2

Ask

The mapper creates questions about permission, capacity, projection, stalls, transport and local value.

3

Verify

Hosts, ferry operators, stallholders and community contacts check what is true and what is still open.

4

Notice

Confirmed public details move into noticeboard feeds, screens, QR codes or social distribution.

Question layers

The pattern stays customisable.

Venue

  • Who can say yes?
  • Who should be consulted early?
  • What already works at this place?

Projection

  • Which surface, power and brightness level fits?
  • What content needs review?
  • How does pack-down work after dark?

Shared Table

  • Is there a food-sharing role?
  • Where are prep, water, bins and storage?
  • Which evidence helps the next pilot?

Transport

  • Which boat and bus windows fit?
  • How do day visitors leave well?
  • Can partner messaging spread arrivals?

Noticeboard

  • What can be public today?
  • Who verifies updates?
  • When should the notice expire?

Learning

  • What changed after the pilot?
  • What made the venue's life easier?
  • Which question should be asked next?
