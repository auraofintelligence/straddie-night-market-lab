Visitor flow

Boat choices are part of the event design.

The useful partnership ask is simple: can SeaLink, Gold Cats / Stradbroke Flyer, buses and venues help map visitor flow so people choose the least stressful boat, bus, walking route and return window?

Partnership questions

What to ask SeaLink and Gold Cats.

The aim is aggregate planning information, better public guidance and lighter pressure on venues.

Before the event

  • Which ferry windows are usually full during the target season?
  • Which arrivals connect cleanly with buses to Point Lookout or Amity?
  • Can a QR question help visitors choose day trip, overnight or local-only paths?

During the event

  • Can public notices suggest lower-pressure boat choices?
  • Can venue signs show last-return and bus reminders?
  • Can ferry terminals display only verified, non-hype market information?

After the event

  • What did visitors actually choose?
  • Where did flow feel too tight, late or confusing?
  • Which next market window would reduce pressure and support the host?

Transport planning should reduce pressure and support host-led events.
